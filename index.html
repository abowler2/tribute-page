Ruth Bader Ginsburg

“Fight for the things that you care about,
but do it in a way that will lead others to join you.”

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Here's a time line of Ruth Bader Ginsburg's life:

  • 1933 - Born in Brooklyn, NY
  • 1954 - Graduated from Cornell University
  • 1956 - Enrolled at Harvard Law School where she was one of only nine women in a class of about 500 men.
  • 1959 - After transferring to Columbia University Law School she earns her law degree and finishes tied for fist in her class.
  • 1963 - Started her first position as a professer at Rutgers University.
  • 1972 - Taught at Columbia University, where she became the first tenured woman.
  • 1972 - Co-founded the Women's Rights Project at the American Civil Liberties Union.
  • 1980 - Nominated to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia by President Jimmy Carter
  • 1993 - Nominated to the U.S. Supreme Court by President Bill Clinton, becoming only the 2nd woman justice on the court.
  • 2002 - Inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame.
  • 2010 - Became the oldest justice on the court at the age of 77.
  • present - Remains a justice and intends to do so while she is still mentally sharp enough to perform her duties.
